Electric Job

A utility repair route where players take a work vehicle, accept assignments, and complete maintenance-style tasks.

What it is

The electric job is a utility-repair route where you take a work truck, wait for an assignment, and repair marked problem spots around the city. It is a straightforward legal loop with a strong "drive out, fix it, get sent to the next one" rhythm.

How to get started

Go to the electric job starter, go on duty, spawn the work truck, and get into it. The assignment system waits briefly before handing you the first waypoint, so the key is staying in the job vehicle and letting the route begin.

How it works in practice

Once the script assigns a job, it marks the next repair location. Drive there, interact at the pole or repair point, finish the repair timer, get paid to bank, and then wait for the next assignment to queue.

What to know before you commit

This job is stricter than it looks: the assignment loop expects you to be in the work vehicle when it rolls the next job. If you get stuck, the script also provides a reset/end path instead of leaving you trapped on duty.

What this wiki leaves out on purpose

This is a legal job, so the main thing omitted is low-value internal timing and event details, not secret gameplay.

Practical Flow

How to actually run it

Step 1

Start the electric job and go on duty.

Step 2

Spawn the work truck and get into it.

Step 3

Wait for the system to assign the first repair location.

Step 4

Drive to the marked repair point and press the interact key to start the fix.

Step 5

Finish the repair timer, collect the bank payout, and let the next job roll.

Step 6

Use the built-in cancel/reset option if the route ever gets stuck.
