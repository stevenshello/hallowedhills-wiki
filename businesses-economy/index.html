Businesses & Economy

The economy in Hallowed Hills is built around legal jobs, player-owned businesses, spending, saving, and creating roleplay that actually matters. Whether you want to work a normal job, run a company, or build a small empire, this page covers the basics.

Important: Economy systems are meant to support roleplay, not replace it. Businesses should create interaction, stories, and opportunities for others in the city.

Economy Overview

Money in Hallowed Hills is earned through jobs, services, business ownership, contracts, and player interaction. The goal is to keep the economy active and fair while rewarding players who put effort into their character and roleplay.

Legal Income

Earn money through whitelisted jobs, civilian work, services, deliveries, and player-run business activity.

Business Ownership

Players may have opportunities to own or manage businesses, depending on server availability and staff approval.

Spending & Growth

Vehicles, housing, supplies, and operations all play a role in how your character grows financially over time.

Starting a Business

Businesses should have a purpose in the server and offer something meaningful to the community. A business is more than a money printer with a logo slapped on it like a toddler made a corporation.

  • Have a clear business concept and roleplay purpose.
  • Offer a service, product, or experience that other players can interact with.
  • Keep business activity realistic and active.
  • Follow all server rules and staff expectations.
  • Do not use a business as a front for low-effort or rule-breaking behavior.

Staff may require applications, interviews, or activity checks for certain businesses. Some locations may also be limited depending on demand.

Player-Owned Business Expectations

Be Active

Inactive businesses may be reviewed, reassigned, or removed if they stop contributing to roleplay.

Create RP

Your business should generate scenes, jobs, events, and interaction for more than just yourself.

Be Professional

Managers and owners are expected to handle employees, customers, and disputes with maturity.

Stay Realistic

Prices, pay, and business operations should stay within reasonable server standards.

Jobs & Employment

Not everyone needs to own a business to make money. Many characters build their story through employment, side work, and networking.

  • Take on public or civilian jobs to build starting income.
  • Apply for whitelisted departments or organizations for long-term career roleplay.
  • Work for player-owned businesses as an employee, manager, or contractor.
  • Use roleplay to negotiate pay, contracts, and opportunities.

Economic Rules

  • No exploiting, duping, glitching, or abusing game systems for money or items.
  • No unrealistic pricing meant to break or bypass the economy.
  • No fake businesses created only to funnel money with no actual roleplay.
  • No staff-shopping or arguing after final decisions on business approvals.
  • All business and economic activity must follow the server rules at all times.

Tips for Success

Network

Talk to people. Most opportunities come from connections, not just menus and paychecks.

Stay Consistent

Reliable characters and active business owners usually grow faster than people chasing quick money.

Think Long-Term

Build a reputation, not just a bank balance. The best economy roleplay creates stories that stick.

Need Help?

If you have questions about business ownership, economic systems, or approvals, reach out through the appropriate community support channels or review any linked application pages if available.
