Businesses & Economy

Want to build a business, make money, hire employees, or grow your character’s place in the city? This page covers how businesses, jobs, income, and the economy work in Hallowed Hills Roleplay.

Overview Business Types Ownership Economy Rules Tips for Success

Overview

The economy is meant to support roleplay, create opportunity, and reward active players who contribute to the city.

Businesses should create meaningful roleplay for more than just the owner. If it only exists to print money, it ain’t a business — it’s a scam with better branding.

Player-Owned Business Ownership

Owning a business is a bigger responsibility than just having a cool sign and acting like the CEO of vibes.

Clear Purpose

Your business should offer a real service, product, or experience that players can interact with.

Active Roleplay

Businesses should stay active and consistently create scenes, jobs, and opportunities for others.

Professional Conduct

Owners and managers are expected to handle staffing, disputes, and business activity maturely.

Server Value

A good business adds life to the city and gives other players reasons to show up and participate.

Inactive, unrealistic, or low-effort businesses may be reviewed, restricted, or removed. Don’t open a whole empire just to disappear like a haunted tax write-off.

Common Business Ideas

Depending on server setup and approvals, players may be able to operate or work in businesses like these.

Restaurants & Cafés

Create food service roleplay, host events, and provide a social spot for the city.

Mechanic Shops

Handle repairs, towing, upgrades, and vehicle-related roleplay for the driving community.

Retail & Services

Run stores, security services, entertainment venues, or other roleplay-friendly operations.

Custom Concepts

If allowed, unique business ideas may be pitched to staff for approval based on quality and fit.

Economy Rules

The server economy should stay fair, realistic, and healthy for the whole community.

1. No exploiting

Do not abuse bugs, glitches, dupes, or loopholes to gain money, items, or business advantage.

2. Keep pricing realistic

Prices, wages, and charges should make sense within the server economy and not be wildly inflated.

3. Businesses must be active

If you own a business, you are expected to actually use it and create roleplay through it.

4. Follow all server rules

Business ownership does not excuse powergaming, favoritism, staff issues, or rulebreaking.

Some businesses may require staff approval, activity checks, interviews, or additional guidelines depending on the type of operation.

Tips for Success

  • Be consistent and stay active.
  • Create roleplay for employees, customers, and the community.
  • Build a reputation, not just a bank account.
  • Keep your operations realistic and organized.
  • Think long-term when growing your character or your business.
